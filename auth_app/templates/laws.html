Legal Rights and Acts in India

Guardians & Wards Act, 1890
This law governs the appointment of a guardian for a minor child, ensuring the welfare of the child in terms of custody and care, subject to court approval.
Indian Penal Code, 1860
The IPC is a comprehensive statute that outlines criminal law in India, detailing offenses and their corresponding punishments, including crimes related to theft, murder, and sexual offenses.
Christian Marriage Act, 1872
This Act regulates the marriage and divorce procedures of Christians in India, ensuring the legal recognition of Christian marriages and providing guidelines for the dissolution of such marriages.
Indian Evidence Act, 1872
This Act governs the law of evidence in Indian courts and provides the rules regarding the admissibility of evidence during judicial proceedings.
Married Women's Property Act, 1874
This Act ensures that a married woman has the right to own and manage her property, free from control by her husband or any third party.
Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923
This Act provides compensation to workers who are injured or killed in the course of their employment, covering medical costs, lost wages, and more.
Indian Successions Act, 1925
This Act governs the law of inheritance and succession, specifying how property is distributed among heirs after the death of a person.
Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956
It aims to combat human trafficking, particularly prostitution, by criminalizing the act of keeping brothels and trafficking for sexual exploitation.
Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961
This Act makes the giving or taking of dowry illegal and punishes those who demand or accept dowry in any form during the marriage process.
Commission of Sati (Prevention) Act, 1987
The Act criminalizes the practice of sati (widow immolation) and provides strict penalties for those involved in or encouraging such practices.
Cinematograph Act, 1952
It regulates the certification and censorship of films in India, ensuring that they conform to societal standards and legal guidelines before public screening.
Births, Deaths & Marriages Registration Act, 1886
This law mandates the registration of births, deaths, and marriages in India, providing official recognition and records for these life events.
Minimum Wages Act, 1948
This Act ensures that workers are paid a minimum wage for their labor, aiming to prevent exploitation and ensure fair compensation in various industries.
Prevention of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012
It specifically addresses offenses against children, making sexual abuse of minors a punishable crime and setting strict punishments for offenders.
Child Marriage Restraint Act, 1929
This Act prohibits the marriage of minors in India, establishing the minimum legal age for marriage to prevent child marriages and protect the rights of children.
Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1937
This law applies Muslim personal law to Muslims in India in matters of marriage, inheritance, and other personal affairs, according to Islamic principles.
Indecent Representation of Women (Prevention) Act, 1986
It prohibits the indecent or obscene representation of women in advertisements, publications, or any media form, aiming to protect women’s dignity.
Special Marriage Act, 1954
It allows individuals of different religions or faiths to marry and provides a legal framework for such marriages, as well as matters related to divorce and inheritance.
Hindu Marriage Act, 1955
This law governs the marriage, divorce, and adoption practices among Hindus in India, establishing legal norms for the institution of marriage within Hinduism.
Hindu Succession Act, 1956
It outlines the inheritance and succession rules for Hindus, determining how property is passed down among family members after a person’s death.
Foreign Marriage Act, 1969
This Act regulates the marriages of Indian citizens abroad, ensuring that marriages conducted in foreign countries are legally valid in India.
Family Courts Act, 1984
It provides for the establishment of family courts to resolve matrimonial disputes, child custody, and other family-related matters in a speedy and amicable manner.
Maternity Benefit Act, 1961
This law ensures that women receive paid maternity leave, with provisions for job protection and financial support during pregnancy and after childbirth.
Hindu Adoption & Maintenance Act, 1956
It governs the adoption and maintenance of children in Hindu families, setting rules on the eligibility of adoptive parents and the welfare of adopted children.
Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973
This law establishes the procedures for the investigation, trial, and punishment of criminal offenses in India, ensuring the due process of law in criminal cases.
Medical Termination of Pregnancy Act, 1971
It legalizes abortion in India under specific conditions, providing safe and regulated procedures for terminating pregnancies under certain medical and social conditions.
National Commission for Women Act, 1990
This Act establishes the National Commission for Women (NCW) to protect and promote the rights of women, address their issues, and recommend policies for their welfare.
Pre-conception and Pre-natal Diagnostic Techniques (Prohibition of Sex Selection) Act, 1994
This law prohibits the use of prenatal diagnostic techniques for sex determination to prevent sex-selective abortion and promote gender equality.
Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005
It provides legal protection to women against domestic violence, including physical, emotional, and economic abuse, and ensures safety and support for victims.
Sexual Harassment of Women at Work Place (Prevention, Prohibition & Redressal) Act, 2013
It mandates workplace policies to prevent and address sexual harassment of women, establishing mechanisms for complaints and redressal.
Indian Divorce Act, 1969
This Act governs the divorce proceedings for Christians in India, providing grounds for divorce, separation, and maintenance under Christian law.
Equal Remuneration Act, 1976
It ensures that men and women are paid equally for the same work or work of similar nature, aiming to eliminate gender-based pay disparities.
